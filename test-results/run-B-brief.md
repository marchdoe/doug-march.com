# Signals Brief — 2026-03-16

## Palette Direction
Overcast spring — warm enough to feel the season turning but cloaked in grey. Muted greens and soft, damp earth tones with the saturation dialed back, like a March day that can't decide whether to bloom or brood. Not cold, not bright — humid and held.

## Layout Energy
Monday. Structured, purposeful, no wasted space. 11.7 hours of daylight means we're gaining light but not swimming in it — the layout should feel workmanlike and gridded but not suffocating. Tight margins, clear hierarchy, no laziness.

## Tension
Tomorrow is St. Patrick's Day, which wants to party, but the new moon and that Lao Tzu quote about emptiness and stillness are pulling hard in the opposite direction. The market is down. The sky is grey. Instruct the designer to hold this: the eve-of-celebration anticipation pressed flat under a meditative, overcast stillness. The green wants in but hasn't been let through the door yet.

- **Music** (Guided by Voices, My Morning Jacket, Wet Leg): Lo-fi indie energy — slightly rough, slightly playful, never polished. Typography could lean a touch imperfect, hand-set, like a photocopied zine cover rather than a Swiss grid.
- **Lunar phase** (new moon, 2.3% illumination): Near-total darkness. Reinforce the stillness and restraint. The design should feel like it's holding its breath. Minimal ornamentation.
- **Quote** (Lao Tzu — "Empty yourself of everything"): This resonates hard with the new moon and overcast sky. Let it anchor the atmosphere — the brief wants whitespace and quiet even inside the Monday structure.
- **Hacker News** ("The 49MB web page" at 603 points): There's irony available here — a site that redesigns itself daily nodding at web bloat. Could subtly influence a lean, minimal approach today.

## Anchor Signal
The new moon. When someone lands today, they should feel stillness first — a site holding its breath on a grey Monday, spring barely visible, the party one day away but not yet here.
