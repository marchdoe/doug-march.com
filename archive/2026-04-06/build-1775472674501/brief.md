## Claude's Rationale

The Stack archetype perfectly encodes the brief's layered signals through horizontal band logic: each full-width section becomes a discrete atmospheric moment that honors both Monday's structural clarity and spring's fresh breath. The primary neutral hue (195°) reads as diffuse April morning light—neither warm nor stark, but genuinely cool and misty. The spring sage accent (138°, `#4A9A6C`) appears sparingly in labels and links, encoding growth without saturation. The Featured Work band at `min-height: 55vh` with `padding: 96px 48px` makes daylight itself structural—the longest day of the brief's window deserves the most space. Typography pairs DM Sans (geometric, quiet authority) with IBM Plex Sans (rational warmth), letting careful letter-spacing and weight contrast encode the Radiohead/War on Drugs precision without affectation. Body text contrast is deliberately 6.8:1, honoring the waning gibbous's fading illumination rather than demanding maximum punch. The Melville quote becomes Band 4's leading element, supported by a soft 2px left rule in sage, treating Doug's philosophy as the editorial anchor it deserves to be.
