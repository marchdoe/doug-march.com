# Signals Brief — 2026-03-29

# Creative Brief: doug-march.com
**Date:** March 29, 2026 | **Signal Influence: 5/10** (Moderate)

---

## Mood

Sunday afternoon light, 89% illuminated moon overhead—the site should feel introspective but not heavy. Spring is arriving (Easter seven days out), so there's forward momentum, but the mood is contemplative rather than celebratory. There's a quiet tension here: the weekend calls for spaciousness and ease, yet the Hacker News feed carries weight (GitLab founder's cancer story at 1128 points dominates the cultural temperature), and Detroit sports split the day (Pistons won, Red Wings and Tigers lost). Hold this: the layout should breathe and relax *into* that weight rather than escape it. Don't make it cheerful to compensate for the loss. Let spaciousness be a container for real things.

---

## Composition Direction

**Scroll with Specimen moments.**

Today's signals resist a single rigid structure—the weekend asks for flow, but the thoughtful news cycle and golf tournament underway suggest moments of pause and focus. A primary Scroll direction (letting content breathe vertically, following the moon phase's gradual illumination arc) punctuated by Specimen-like focus on a single editorial moment—likely the HN story or the golf leaderboard—allows the designer to honor both the relaxed energy of Sunday and the weight of what's actually happening in the world.

---

## Typography Direction

Generous, warm letter-spacing and line-height—Sunday calls for air around words. Headings should speak in a moderate voice, not whisper, not shout. Consider a serif/sans pairing that feels slightly nostalgic (resonant with the Zafon quote about giving up great expectations—there's a literary, reflective quality to today). Type should feel unhurried. Avoid compressed, urgent pacing.

---

## Signal Integration

- **Hacker News cultural weight** → The GitLab founder story (1128 points) is the day's strongest signal. Feature a single callout or editorial note acknowledging resilience/persistence—not as a card, but as a quiet statement. This should be FELT most strongly upon landing. Tone: respectful, substantive, not saccharine.

- **Texas Children's Houston Open (golf)** → Gary Woodland leading at -18. Include the leaderboard as a live Specimen moment—a narrow, focused vertical element that contrasts with the scroll. Update status visible. This adds texture without dominating.

- **Lunar phase (waxing gibbous, 89.1% illumination)** → Increase contrast slightly across the page. Let shadows deepen and highlights pop—we're near full moon, so the site should feel lit from within. Subtle, but real.

- **Spring season + approaching Easter (7 days)** → Introduce a single color accent or tonal shift that suggests renewal without being literal. Not Easter eggs; something more muted. A warm undertone in the secondary palette. Restraint.

- **My Morning Jacket / Tobin Sprout / Guided by Voices** → Loose, generous letter-spacing in body text. These are artists with warmth and slight imprecision—let the typography reflect that ease. Avoid overly mechanical or grid-locked pacing.

- **Zafon quote: "We all give up great expectations along the way."** → Consider embedding this as a quiet typographic moment (not a card). Place it where it can breathe. It resonates with the reflective mood of Sunday and the weight of the news cycle.

- **Detroit sports (mixed results)** → Pistons win (+109-87) and Red Wings/Tigers losses should be noted without fanfare. No celebratory badges. Treat wins and losses with equal editorial distance—just scorecard clarity. They're context, not the story.

- **Sunday (weekend energy)** → Layout density should be relaxed. Plenty of white space. Vertical rhythm should feel like pacing a walk, not rushing a task.

---

## Palette Direction

Spring freshness tempered by the introspection of a waxing gibbous moon and a contemplative news cycle. Warm but not saturated—think early-spring palette (soft greens, warm whites, ochre undertones) rather than mid-spring brightness. Contrast should be moderate-to-high (the moon phase calls for it), but the overall saturation should be restrained. Avoid primary colors; lean into earth tones and soft, warm neutrals. The palette should feel like a Sunday morning that knows something serious is being carried.

Creative risk is balanced—be intentional about holding tension, not resolving it.
