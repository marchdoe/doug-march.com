# Signals Brief — 2026-04-07

# Creative Brief: doug-march.com
**Date:** Tuesday, April 7, 2026

---

## Mood

Tuesday morning in early spring, caught between momentum and stasis. The site should feel *attentive* — watching multiple things at once (golf leaderboards tightening, Pistons and Tigers both stumbling, the Masters arriving in two days) without urgency. There's a tension here worth holding: spring is arriving with genuine warmth and lengthening light, but Detroit's teams are losing and the solar calendar is only halfway through daylight hours. The design should feel *awake but measured* — bright enough to acknowledge the season, restrained enough to honor the losses. Don't resolve this. Let it sit.

---

## Composition Direction

**Broadsheet with Specimen tension.**

Today demands the editorial clarity of a newspaper layout (multiple stories competing for attention: the Valero Texas Open is final, the Masters approaches in 48 hours, local teams are stumbling) but executed with the *precision and white space* of a Specimen. The golf narrative is the spine, but it should coexist with other elements as *distinct objects*, not blended into a feed. This matches the signal weight: nothing dominates, but everything deserves its own space and gravity.

---

## Typography Direction

Use a strict, measured hierarchy with generous internal spacing. Headings should speak clearly but without shouting—this is Tuesday, not Friday. Letter-spacing should be controlled and slightly tight (influenced by Radiohead's angular precision in the rotation), but word-spacing should be generous to match the broadsheet clarity. Type scale should differentiate sections dramatically, but use whitespace, not size alone, as the primary organizational tool. The body should feel spacious and readable—spring light is real, and the design should honor it with air.

---

## Signal Integration

- **Golf (Valero Texas Open final, leaderboard active) → Primary editorial element.** J.J. Spaun at -17 with three players within one shot is a tight finish worth displaying as a live leaderboard widget or card. Position this prominently but not dominantly—use the Broadsheet/Specimen tension to give it its own column or section rather than hero placement. The narrative is "precision under pressure," which mirrors the Tuesday mood.

- **Masters Tournament (2 days away) → Subtle countdown element, understated.** Not a banner. A small, type-only reference in the navigation or sidebar acknowledging the approach. This is *anticipation*, not hype. Let it whisper.

- **Detroit teams (Pistons loss 107-123, Tigers loss 3-7) → Dismissive inline scorelines, not featured.** These should appear as small factual callouts—perhaps in a dedicated "Results" section or sidebar—with a tone of acknowledgment but no editorial celebration. No badges, no color coding. Just the numbers, stated plainly. The loss is real, but it's Tuesday; move forward.

- **Daylight hours (12.7 hours) → Light palette anchor.** We're at mid-spring length now—use this to justify a brighter, more open layout with higher contrast. This is not yet the long days of summer, so don't overexpose. But the design should *feel* lit.

- **Spring season → Palette saturation and warmth baseline.** Fresh greens are possible but should be restrained. Think spring on a cloudy April morning in the Midwest: alive but not saturated. Subtle warm undertones (ochre, soft gold) are permissible as accents, not dominants.

- **Hacker News (Ghost Pepper at 384 points, GPU history at 90, rice farming personal essay at 166) → Tech-forward personality texture.** The breadth here (hardware, agriculture, speech-to-text, AI) signals *curiosity across domains*. Let this inform the typographic character—use subtle code-aesthetic hints (monospace for certain callouts, precise alignment) but don't overstate it. Felt, not seen.

- **Music (Radiohead, Wet Leg, Tobin Sprout) → Angular, precise letter-spacing and type personality.** Radiohead's meticulous production should inform tightness in spacing; Wet Leg's wit should inform tone in any editorial micro-copy. Tobin Sprout (lo-fi, intimate) might permit slight imprecision or warmth in type treatment. Use this as a *personality filter* for how the type breathes.

- **Lunar phase (last quarter, 67% illuminated) → Mild contrast reduction.** We're descending from the full moon. Don't muddy things, but pull back from maximum contrast slightly. A waning moon permits softer, slightly lower-contrast typography and subtle reduction in accent color intensity.

- **Quote ("Better to live until you die") → Potential anchor if it appears organically.** This is a good quote—pragmatic, slightly dry, fitting for a Tuesday. If it finds a natural home (perhaps paired with the approaching Masters or the day's results), feature it briefly. But don't force it. Only if the composition naturally permits.

---

## Palette Direction

**Warm, restrained, spring-lit, with high legibility.** Base the palette in soft greens and warm grays (influenced by April and 12.7 hours of daylight), but resist saturation—think new grass at dawn, not summer lawn. Introduce warm accents (soft ochre, pale gold) sparingly, reserved for highlights or editorial moments (the golf leaderboard, the Masters countdown). Maintain high contrast between text and background to honor the Broadsheet clarity and the lengthening days. The overall feeling should be *fresh but not cheerful*—spring with editorial restraint. The waning moon permits a slight reduction in accent color intensity, but the primary palette should feel lit, not muted.
