# Creative Brief — doug-march.com
**Date:** 2026-04-20 | **Signal Influence: 5/10**

---

## Mood

Monday morning in spring: early light, steady momentum, a quiet tension between aspiration and self-awareness. The site should breathe with the season's optimism but ground itself in something real — there's no major news to celebrate, no weather drama, just the steady hum of a productive week beginning. A waxing crescent moon suggests things are gathering energy, not yet full. Hold the duality here: fresh and forward-looking, but not naive. The tech community (Hacker News, GitHub signals) is in a skeptical mood about authenticity — fake stars, security breaches, nostalgia for simpler systems. Let that skepticism live quietly in the design without freezing it. This is a day for clarity and intentional restraint, not spectacle.

---

## Composition Direction

**Specimen with Gallery Wall energy.** 

Today calls for a layout that treats the portfolio itself as a curated collection of discrete objects — each project breathing in its own space, arranged with intention rather than flow. The Specimen archetype's focus on isolation and careful framing suits a Monday when focus is high and distractions are few. Gallery Wall energy prevents it from feeling sterile; projects should feel like they're in dialogue with each other through negative space and visual rhythm, not connected by narrative. This matches the awwwards references (Daniel Destefanis, House of Adjaycent) which both favor compositional precision over decorative connective tissue.

---

## Typography Direction

Tight, deliberate, quiet authority. Headings should whisper rather than shout — use scale sparingly and let generous negative space do the work of hierarchy. Justified or carefully tracked body text; letter-spacing should be precise but not loose. The waxing crescent and early-week momentum suggest typography that feels controlled and intentional, not relaxed. Avoid rounded forms; lean toward geometric or classical sans with clean angles. This reflects the skepticism in today's tech discourse (GitHub's fake star economy, security concerns) — the design should feel like it knows what it's doing, no performance, no excess.

## Palette Direction

Cool-to-neutral foundation with selective warm accents. Spring saturation is present but restrained — think sage, soft olive, warm grays rather than bright greens. High contrast is not needed today; instead, rely on contrast through value shift and careful color placement. The overall feeling should be clean and intentional, never muddy but never stark. Warm whites and soft neutrals keep the space breathable; a single warm accent color (clay, rust, or warm sand) can appear sparingly in project callouts or metadata. The waxing crescent and early daylight hours allow for lighter backgrounds without feeling washed out. Avoid cool blues or purples — they'd feel too evening-focused. This is morning light, not night-time drama.
