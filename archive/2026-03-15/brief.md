## Claude's Rationale

March 15 — the Ides of March. Caesar's assassination, the most famous betrayal in history. The design channels carved Roman monumentalism: marble and blood. The palette is built on oxblood crimson against bone-white and deep volcanic stone. Typography is pushed to extremes — massive display sizes for titles that feel like inscriptions on a senate wall, with tight letter-spacing that compresses like a crowd closing in. Borders are sharp and deliberate, like blade edges. The layout breathes with dramatic vertical space, monumental and exposed, as if each section is a marble slab laid bare. The tension lives in the contrast between the serene, classical spacing and the violence of the red accent — beauty and danger occupying the same frame.
