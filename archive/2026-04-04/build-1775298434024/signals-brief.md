# CREATIVE BRIEF: doug-march.com

## Mood

Saturday morning light breaking into spring — a moment of pause before tomorrow's Easter. The day carries a contemplative ease (it's the weekend, daylight is generous at 12.6 hours, the moon is nearly full), but there's an undercurrent of anticipation and incompleteness. Thich Nhat Hanh's "Smile, breathe and go slowly" whispers through this, not as a forced mantra but as the natural rhythm of a day caught between seasons and between action and rest. Let this tension breathe: the site should feel spacious and calm, yet alert — not lazy, not frantic, but *present*. The approaching holiday (Easter tomorrow) and Masters (5 days out) are on the periphery, felt but not loudly announced.

---

## Composition Direction

**Primary: Scroll | Secondary: Specimen energy**

A scrolling page allows the eye to move downward with the rhythm of the day itself — unhurried, generative. But within that scroll, treat individual elements (a project, a note, a data visualization) with Specimen-like clarity and intentionality: each item should feel *placed*, not flowing. This hybrid respects the weekend's spaciousness (Scroll) while maintaining the craft-forward, human-centered sensibility of today's Awwwards references (The Empty Glass, The Tawaraya — both of which privilege singular, well-lit moments over frenzy).

---

## Typography Direction

Generous and rhythmic. Leading should be ample — this is a breathing day, not a dense one. Headings should speak in a conversational register, not bark. Use moderate contrast between sizes; avoid extremes. Letter-spacing can be natural and open (not tight, not sprawling) — think of Wet Leg's confident slackness rather than technical precision. Hierarchy should emerge through size, weight, and placement, not through color or distortion. The overall feel: calm authority, not shouty urgency.

## Signal Integration

- **Easter (tomorrow, +1 day)** → Subtle threshold marker in the upper navigation or as a thin, quiet divider. No iconography, no color shift. Just the word "Easter" in context, understated, acknowledging the next chapter without overshadowing today. Treat it as a gentle waypoint, not a campaign.

- **Waning gibbous moon (91.7% illumination)** → Increase overall contrast subtly. The page should feel well-lit, almost *bright* — as if the nearly-full moon is lending its luminosity to the daytime. Use deeper blacks in type and accent elements; let whites breathe. This is not a dark day.

- **Saturday / Weekend energy** → Layout density drops. Spacing between sections increases. Margins expand. No dense grids or card layouts. Each element should have room to exist without competing for attention.

- **Spring season + 12.6 hours daylight** → Palette is fresh and unsaturated but *alive*. Not muted, not pastel — spring greens and earth tones with high luminosity. The page should feel like midday light coming through a window, not filtered or dimmed.

- **Golf: Valero Texas Open (in progress, MacIntyre -14)** → If referenced at all, a *very* small leaderboard snippet in a secondary location (footer, sidebar, not hero). This is golf weather, but not Masters-week gravity. Acknowledge with restraint.

- **"Smile, breathe and go slowly" (Thich Nhat Hanh)** → This quote should appear somewhere prominent but not as a banner. Embed it naturally — as a page section header, a pull-quote within a project description, or as accompanying text to a visual element. Its tone of mindfulness should *influence* the pacing of the page (don't rush the reader through the scroll), not be shouted.

- **Awwwards references (imagery-led, seasonal, interactive subtlety)** → Favor composition that lets visual content (photography, data viz) lead. Navigation should be minimal and intuitive. Interactions should be *felt* (hovering changes subtle things — contrast, spacing, opacity) rather than announced. Follow The Tawaraya's approach: let the visuals breathe; the frame is quiet.

- **GitHub activity (TypeScript, Python, Rust — tech stack breadth)** → If Doug's work is displayed, signal the diversity of languages through subtle visual distinction (icon, small label, muted color accent) without making it feel like a stats dashboard. Let it breathe as context, not callout.

## Palette Direction

**High-luminosity spring palette with moderate saturation and strong contrast.** Anchor in warm, earthy neutrals (soft cream, warm gray, natural wood tones) with fresh accent hues — sage green, warm terracotta, soft gold — all at medium saturation, not oversaturated pastels. Contrast should be **high** (the waning gibbous moon effect): deep charcoal or black type against light backgrounds, and vice versa. Avoid muddy or heavily desaturated tones; everything should feel *lit* and *clear*, as if photographed in golden-hour diffuse daylight. The palette should feel like spring isn't shouting, but has arrived and is calmly, confidently present.
