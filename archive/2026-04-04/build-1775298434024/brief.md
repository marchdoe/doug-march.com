**Design Brief:** Spring arrived, nearly full moon, Saturday pause—a scrolling moment of spacious clarity where each element sits placed and lit, breathing between sections, guided by the quiet instruction to smile, breathe, and go slowly.

## Claude's Rationale

Doug March's portfolio demanded a light-mode spring palette with warm earthy anchors—the first light design in four days. Primary hue 42° (golden straw) generates a parchment neutral scale that reads as midday spring light through a window: luminous yet grounded. The accent is sage green (hue 110°) at moderate saturation—fresh without pastels. Typography pairs Playfair Display (editorial warmth, conversational register) with Source Sans 3 (open, readable, unhurried). Major Third scale (1.250) provides moderate contrast suited to a Scroll archetype with Specimen moments. The design encodes five signals—Easter, waning gibbous moon (as brightness), weekend spacing, spring clarity, and the Thich Nhat Hanh quote—into a spacious, contemplative layout where elements breathe rather than compete. High contrast (14:1 on body text) reflects the moon's luminosity directive; generous leading (1.65 line-height) honors the Saturday pause; accent green appears sparingly (quote rule, first project label) to maintain restraint per brief.
