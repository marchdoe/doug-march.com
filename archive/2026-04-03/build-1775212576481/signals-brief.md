# Signals Brief — 2026-04-03

# Creative Brief: doug-march.com
## April 3, 2026

---

## Mood

Spring Friday afternoon—the light is long and the energy is forward-facing, but there's a restless quality to today. The full moon at 96% illumination brings contrast and clarity, while the awwwards references whisper that image-driven, subtle-interaction design is having a moment. Hold the tension between the relaxed Friday energy and the precise, almost architectural restraint visible in the winning sites. Don't soften into weekend leisure; instead, let Friday's structural clarity meet spring's renewal. The result should feel like a portfolio that *knows* what it's showing you, with no wasted movement.

---

## Composition Direction

**Gallery Wall with Specimen undertones.**

Today's reference sites (especially The Gum and The Tawaraya) prioritize imagery as the primary navigation and editorial voice—they're curated collections, not linear narratives. A Gallery Wall archetype respects this image-forward signal. The Specimen undercurrent arrives via the full moon's demand for contrast and clarity; individual pieces should have breathing room and deliberate white space around them, not a packed salon hang. This hybrid lets work speak loudly while maintaining the Friday restraint that says "each thing here matters separately."

---

## Typography Direction

Generous and precise. Type should *breathe*—loose letter-spacing, generous line-height, ample margins around text blocks—but maintain strict hierarchy through weight and scale alone, never color or ornament. Headings should whisper rather than shout; they're organizational signposts, not declarations. Let the imagery carry the emotional weight. The angular precision of Radiohead's influence and the full moon's high-contrast demand should manifest as clean, unadorned sans-serif with tight tracking only in small, functional text (dates, labels, callouts). Nothing friendly or rounded today.

---

## Signal Integration

- **Full Moon (96% illumination) → High-contrast, sculptural lighting on all imagery.** Shadows should be deliberate and dark; highlights should be bright. No soft, diffused treatment. The page should feel like it's being lit by moonlight—dramatic without being theatrical.

- **Spring season + 12.5 daylight hours → Palette draws from fresh greens and earth tones, but treated with restraint.** Not pastel, not saturated. Think early-April garden—muted sage, soft clay, pale cream. The long daylight should mean airy spacing and generous whitespace; the page feels less dense than a winter brief.

- **Friday (day_index: 5) → Structural clarity without weekend sprawl.** Friday is organized and forward-facing. Navigation and hierarchy should be crisp. No lazy, exploratory wandering. The user should understand the information architecture immediately.

- **Awwwards imagery-led design trend → Prioritize photography/visual work in the composition.** Text should serve images, not the reverse. If portfolio projects are shown, let them dominate the viewport. Interactions should be subtle—hover states, gentle transitions—not playful or obvious.

- **Detroit sports wins (Red Wings 4-2, Pistons 113-108) → Minimal editorial acknowledgment, tucked as a small, matter-of-fact callout.** Not celebratory; treated like a scoreboard update. Inline, brief, factual tone. This is context, not content.

- **Radiohead + Tobin Sprout + Wet Leg on rotation → Angular, precise typographic personality.** These artists share a preference for clarity and edge over warmth. Type spacing should be tight in callouts; rhythmic and structured in longer passages.

- **Paulo Coelho quote ("Dreams provide nourishment...") → Include but subordinate.** The quote resonates with spring renewal and the lunar cycle, but it should not be the visual hero. Feature it as a quiet, single-line element—perhaps near a project heading or tucked as a subtle atmospheric element. Treat it as context, not declaration.

- **Valero Texas Open (golf) + Easter approaching in 2 days → Acknowledge Easter lightly as thematic undertone, ignore golf leaderboard.** Easter is close enough to register as minor seasonal tension, but it shouldn't drive design. No bunnies, no pastels. Just the subtlest hint of renewal/resurrection in how transitions and micro-interactions reveal new content.

---

## Palette Direction

Cool-warm balance with emphasis on restraint and contrast. Primary palette: soft, desaturated earth tones (sage, taupe, pale clay) layered against a near-white background. The full moon demands high contrast, so use deep charcoal or nearly-black for text and shadows—this creates drama without saturation. Spring's freshness should arrive through the *absence* of gray, not through bright greens or pastels. Accent with one warm tone (soft rust or ochre) used sparingly—perhaps in a border, a thin line, or a small detail that draws the eye to key work. Overall feeling: sophisticated, slightly cool, with just enough warmth to prevent coldness. Muted but not dull; precise but not clinical.
