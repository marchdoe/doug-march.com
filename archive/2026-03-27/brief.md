## Claude's Rationale

The Gallery Wall archetype directly embodies the brief's core tension: a space where most work is visible at once, yet certain pieces merit elevated presence. Rather than a rigid grid, asymmetric cell spans and deliberate whitespace gaps create visual breathing room that mirrors the "in transit" quality—nothing resolves into expected symmetry. The color strategy starts from cool slate (210° hue) to encode technical precision and control-room aesthetics, paired with a restrained spring yellow-green accent at 82° saturation 60%, chosen because March 27 is only 26 days into spring, before full chlorophyll confidence arrives. The seafoam secondary accent appears exactly once—a 2px left border on one featured cell—as a knowing nod to the day's HN story about calming industrial control rooms, a gesture rather than decoration. Typography pairs Space Grotesk's geometric, slightly quirky personality with DM Sans's clean geometry; both chosen for technical precision without warmth, letting the palette carry seasonality. The Aristophanes quote occupies the far-right grid edge, rotated 90° against the gallery's left-to-right flow, embedding resistance directly into the layout—a structural argument against Friday's forward momentum. Spacing follows Gallery Wall guidance: 16px gaps between clusters (Friday-hinge density), 24px cell padding. The waxing gibbous moon's crescendo is encoded as elevated contrast—background #111C28 (deeper than standard dark mode) paired with text #EEF2F8 for ~14:1 ratio—felt in the composition's crispness, never annotated.
