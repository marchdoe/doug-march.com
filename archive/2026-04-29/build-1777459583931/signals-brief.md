# Signals Brief — 2026-04-29

# Creative Brief: doug-march.com
**April 29, 2026 | Wednesday | Full Moon (96.5% illumination)**

---

## Mood

A night of maximum visibility meets the midweek grind. Spring's renewal energy is here, but it's Wednesday—the moon is nearly full and bright enough to cast shadows, yet we're in the thick of work, not celebration. There's a productive tension today: the Hacker News conversation around decentralization and platform independence (Ghostty leaving GitHub, Radicle adoption) collides with the collaborative, audience-first thinking in today's Awwwards sites. Build the design to feel both luminous and focused—bright but not festive, clear but not sparse. Let the full moon's contrast live alongside the quiet competence of a mid-week portfolio review.

---

## Composition Direction

**Broadsheet with Specimen moments.**

Today calls for editorial clarity and information hierarchy—Broadsheet's strength—but punctuated by moments of extreme detail and close-reading (Specimen). The Awwwards references show sites built for *multiple distinct audiences* (The Push, peace put) and strategic layering (THE UN KNOWN). A Broadsheet structure lets you organize information with columnar clarity while landing specific projects or insights with specimen-level zoom. The full moon's high contrast and Wednesday's midweek focus both favor structured, legible composition over loose exploration.

---

## Typography Direction

Generous vertical breathing but tight, precise horizontal spacing. Headings should whisper authority rather than shout—this is Wednesday professionalism, not a launch day. Let the type scale be generous (the daylight is long at 13.6 hours, and the full moon deserves visual air), but lock down letter-spacing and line-height with confidence. The Radiohead influence should live here: angular precision in tracking and alignment, not in serif/sans choices. Avoid softness; favor clarity and intentional spacing over decoration.

---

## Signal Integration

- **Full moon (96.5% illumination) → High contrast in light/dark balance throughout.** This is the dominant atmospheric signal. Increase separation between foreground and background elements. Shadows should be crisp, highlights bright. Use this to create visual clarity that mirrors the moon's illumination—think stark, readable, almost theatrical. This is felt first when landing on the site.

- **Hacker News: Ghostty/platform independence discourse → Subtle decentralization visual language.** Not heavy-handed. Work the idea of distribution and independence into layout—perhaps asymmetrical composition choices, or elements that don't rely on a central axis. The news cycle is discussing leaving centralized platforms; let that thinking inform how information is organized without making it literal.

- **Awwwards sites: multi-audience design + strategic layering → Implement clear audience segmentation or role-based entry points.** Today's reference sites succeed by serving multiple stakeholders clearly. If doug-march.com surfaces different work for different viewers (technical depth vs. creative direction vs. collaboration examples), this is the day to make those pathways more explicit through composition.

- **Spring season (April, fresh growth) → Restrained green or growth-adjacent accent.** Not aggressive. A single accent color drawn from spring—moss, sage, or muted chartreuse—used sparingly in typography, underlines, or minimal border elements. Saturation should be moderate, not pop. This is texture, not dominance.

- **Detroit Tigers loss (2-5) → Dismissive callout, tucked low or in footer.** If sports results appear on the site, acknowledge the loss with wry editorial voice—brief scoreline, maybe a one-liner with swagger. Not sad, not celebratory. Just a fact stated with personality. Keeps the mood grounded; prevents the design from feeling saccharine.

- **Radiohead + Guided by Voices on rotation → Angular precision in type rendering and alignment.** Radiohead's geometric precision should influence kerning and baseline alignment. Guided by Voices' lo-fi ethos permits imperfection but demands intentionality. The tension between precision (Radiohead) and rawness (GBV) should show up as confident but unstyled typography—no unnecessary flourishes.

- **Oscar Wilde quote ("Whenever people agree with me I always feel I must be wrong") → Optional anchor for contrarian positioning.** If this resonates with the portfolio's voice, feature it briefly—perhaps as a small epigraph or in a footer callout. It's a meta-commentary on taste and independent thinking. Only include if it genuinely reflects how doug-march.com positions itself; don't force it.

## Palette Direction

Cool-to-neutral base (no warm temperature signal today) with high contrast to match the full moon's visibility. Spring saturation is moderate—avoid pastels or washed tones; favor clarity over softness. Think of the palette as *lucid*: clean neutrals (near-white, true gray, deep charcoal) with a single restrained accent in spring green or moss. Contrast should be high throughout—the long daylight (13.6 hours) and near-full moon both permit and reward brightness and shadow separation. Avoid muddy mid-tones; favor legibility and visual separation. This is a cool, clear palette with minimal color noise.
