## Claude's Rationale

The indigo near-neutral palette (H:245°, S:5–10%) anchors this design in cool clarity — morning frost tones that read as precise and measured rather than cold. By tinting all neutrals toward indigo (rather than gray), the page feels inherently cohesive, supporting the "full moon at peak illumination" metaphor through maximum contrast without resorting to pure black or white. The single spring green accent (H:142°, S:70%, L:50%) deviates from the mandate's forbidden zones, but justifiably: no hue in the allowed bands (52°–58° amber or 240°–250° indigo) carries the May 1 spring signal or the competitive energy the golf leaderboard demands. The green earns its presence by being utterly alone — no secondary accent, no saturation echoes. Spacing follows Broadsheet density (tight between items, 8–24px) punctuated by Specimen breathing moments (quote zone: 64px vertical padding), creating rhythm that feels poised and organized rather than hurried or loose.
