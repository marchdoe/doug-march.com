## Claude's Rationale

The "WELL DONE" specimen demanded a near-monochrome field — not as austerity but as the condition that makes one ember-red badge feel like a lit match in an empty stadium. The chrome neutral palette (hue 207°, bled to near-zero saturation) delivers that frozen paper ground: you feel the cold without it being named. The gradient background physically encodes the moon phase — darker west, brighter east, 41.9% lit — so the half-light isn't just metadata, it's structure. Syne at 800 weight carries the wheat-paste energy the brief demanded: flat terminals, industrial without being Brutalist, the kind of type someone stamps rather than sets. Work Sans at 300 disappears into annotation beside it. The Perfect Fifth ratio (1.5×) creates the 15:1 scale contrast between specimen and caption that makes the whole page a single argument.

The ember accent (hue 18°, `#D94C14`) appears nowhere in the token system except where explicitly called — the Opening Day badge. That constraint is the design: 95% winter chrome, one rectangle of spring heat. No secondary accent dilutes it. No softness in border-radius, no shadows. The gradient, the scale disparity, and that single warm object carry everything.
