## Composition Direction

**Scroll with Specimen moments.**

A Scroll structure matches the continuous forward momentum of spring and Tuesday's mid-week rhythm — no heavy landing zone, just a steady descent. But embed 1-2 Specimen-style moments (extreme type scale shifts, isolated focal points) when featuring the Pistons result and the Bob Marley quote. This prevents the scroll from feeling merely linear; it creates pause points that honor small emotional beats without breaking the flow.

## Typography Direction

Generous vertical spacing throughout — let the long daylight hours breathe into the letter-spacing and line-height. Headings should speak with quiet confidence, not shouting; this is Tuesday morning reflection, not Monday urgency. Where type scale shifts dramatically (Specimen moments), let that contrast do the work. The baseline should feel unhurried, almost languid, matching the waxing gibbous phase's sense of patient accumulation.

Think "nearly noon" light rather than "high noon" light. This creates subtle visual tension that mirrors the moon's almost-fullness.

## Palette Direction

**High-contrast spring palette with restrained warmth.** The long daylight hours (13.5) and spring season demand luminosity, but the waxing gibbous moon and Tuesday midweek tone prevent full saturation. Build from a near-white or very light warm gray base, introduce fresh spring accents (celadon, soft sage, pale gold) at moderate saturation — not candy-colored, but alive. Allow shadows and darker moments (for the Pistons moment, for instance) to reach into true darks, creating visual drama without coldness. The overall temperature should be *warm but not hot*, bright but not glaring.
