# Signals Brief — 2026-04-27

# Creative Brief: doug-march.com
**Date:** April 27, 2026 | **Signal Influence:** 5/10 (Moderate)

---

## Mood

Monday morning in late April carries a quiet momentum—spring is now fact, not promise, and the daylight is genuinely generous (13.5 hours). The site should feel alert but not frantic, grounded in the actual season rather than chasing it. There's a tension here worth holding: the Hacker News cycle is spinning with acquisition nostalgia ("I bought Friendster for $30k") and AI philosophy, while the awwwards references showcase polished, purposeful design that's more about *communication* than spectacle. Don't resolve this. Let the portfolio feel both thoughtfully ambitious AND genuinely curious about how things actually work. The waxing gibbous moon (85% lit, day 11 of cycle) suggests we're in the ascending half of a cycle—momentum building, but not yet climax. Lean into that incremental feeling.

---

## Composition Direction

**Scroll with Index undertones.** 

Today's signals and design references suggest a site that *unfolds* progressively rather than announces itself all at once—much like the peace put site's philosophy-inward-and-out approach, and the University of Zlin's interactive timeline. A primary Scroll archetype allows the portfolio to feel like a continuous narrative discovery, but anchor it with Index logic: clear waypoints, readable landmarks, and the sense that you're navigating a *system*, not falling into an abyss. This matches Monday's structured-but-breathing energy and respects the moderate signal weight—design references guide compositional strategy without hijacking it.

---

## Typography Direction

Generous spacing with restrained drama. Headings should speak clearly but not shout; body text should breathe. Tighten letterspacing slightly on accent elements (pulling from Radiohead's angular precision in the music rotation) but keep primary narrative copy open and readable. The day calls for typography that *trusts* its content rather than compensating for thin ideas. Scale variance should exist but earned—hierarchy through proportion and weight, not shock.

## Signal Integration

- **Spring season + 13.5 hours daylight** → The foundational color and light palette should reflect this directly: saturated but not oversaturated greens and earth tones; high-key overall brightness but with intentional shadow zones. This is the PRIMARY visual signal and should be felt immediately upon landing. Think "mid-morning light in a garden" rather than "spring fever."

- **Waxing gibbous moon (85% illumination, day 11)** → Increase overall contrast subtly. The page should feel *lit*—not harsh, but clear. Reduce any muddiness in midtone transitions. This reinforces the sense of momentum without adding visual noise.

- **Hacker News momentum (Friendster resurrection, AI philosophy debates)** → Integrate as editorial texture only: perhaps a small "What's catching people's attention" microcopy element or a sidebar observation, but do NOT make it a feature module. It should feel like overhearing a conversation, not being lectured. Tone: curious, not prescriptive.

- **Awwwards references (peace put philosophy, World of NRG's serious-made-accessible, TBU's interactive history)** → These inform *how* content is *framed*, not what it looks like. Expect the portfolio sections to feel purposeful and philosophical, not decorative. If there are interactive or animated elements, they should communicate something, not just delight.

- **Detroit Tigers win (8-3)** → Light editorial acknowledgment only—perhaps a tiny inline badge or scoreline in a navigation element or footer. Not celebratory, not dismissive. Factual. This is a soft SECONDARY signal that shouldn't dominate but shouldn't be ignored.

- **Monday morning energy** → Layout should feel structured but not rigid. Navigation clear. Information density moderate—breathing room, but not wasted space. This is felt through composition and pacing, not color or decoration.

---

## Palette Direction

A palette rooted in spring saturation but with restraint: rich earth tones (terracotta, deep olive, soft charcoal) as anchors, with fresh greens used as accents rather than dominants. High overall brightness (the 13.5 daylight hours should read as openness, not bleakness), but permit shadows and depth—this isn't flat or pastel. Contrast should be clean and readable but not harsh. A single warm-white or cream as primary background allows the content to land clearly without the page feeling cold. Think less "Easter pastels" and more "soil and new growth." The palette should feel *inhabited*—like a space someone actually works in, not a decoration.
