The full moon and the "quiet scholarship" mood call for elegant restraint; generous line-height and breathing room between elements. Let the Radiohead influence manifest as angular, tight tracking on specific accent moments—but only accents. The baseline should feel like a well-composed printed page, not a screen full of noise.

## Palette Direction

Warm, light-saturated, and spacious. Primary color temperature should feel like late-morning spring light—golden, not harsh. Avoid cool grays; instead use warm beiges, creams, and soft warm whites as neutrals. Saturation should be moderate to full (spring energy, not muted)—permit one accent hue (warm olive, sage, or golden amber) to appear at maybe 3-4 moments on the page. Contrast should be *high at the edges* (type-to-background, accent elements), but midtones should feel soft and unified. The overall impression: a bright, pleasant, uncluttered page that feels like it was designed on a clear Saturday afternoon.
