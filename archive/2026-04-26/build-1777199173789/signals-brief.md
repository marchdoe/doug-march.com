# Signals Brief — 2026-04-26

# Creative Brief — doug-march.com
**Date:** April 26, 2026 | **Signal Influence:** 5/10 (Moderate)

---

## Mood

Sunday morning clarity meeting existential weight. Spring is warm and generous with daylight (13.5 hours), but the quote—*"You came empty handed, and you will leave empty handed"*—lands like a gentle hammer, asking what actually matters. The site should feel reflective without being heavy, spacious without being empty. There's a creative tension here: the season whispers *growth*, but the Bhagavad Gita whispers *impermanence*. Don't resolve this. Let both exist. The design should breathe (spring, weekend, long daylight) while holding something austere (the quote, the waxing gibbous moon still climbing toward fullness).

---

## Composition Direction

**Scroll with Specimen moments.** 

Today calls for a vertical, meditative rhythm—Scroll respects the reflective mood and weekend leisure—but punctuate it with Specimen-scale moments where type or single elements command attention. The Awwwards sites show a tendency toward immersive, journey-based layouts (25 Years of TBU's 3D timeline, peace put's philosophical storytelling). Scroll lets the portfolio breathe vertically while Specimen moments let individual projects or thoughts land with editorial weight. This hybrid honors both the season's expansiveness and the quote's demand for simplicity.

---

## Typography Direction

Generous vertical spacing; restrained character spacing. Headings should *whisper loudly*—large scale but not aggressive, with plenty of whitespace around them. Body type should be set with breathing room between lines (loose leading). The angularity hinted at by the tech-forward GitHub signals (Python, Claude tooling) can inform a slightly geometric typeface choice, but softened—not sharp. Think clarity over cleverness. Weekend energy means no cramped denseness; spring means no dark, tight compositions. Type should feel like it's been given permission to take up space.

## Signal Integration

- **The Bhagavad Gita quote** → Feature once, high on the page, in a moment of visual quiet. Not buried in footer. Large, calm, set with substantial whitespace. This is the day's editorial headline. Tone: contemplative, not mournful.

- **Sunday + Weekend** → Layout should feel unhurried. Sections should have visible breathing room; no card grids or dense layouts. Composition should feel like a slow scroll, not a sprint.

- **Spring + 13.5 daylight hours** → Palette should be luminous and unsaturated. Fresh greens are available but restrained—think sage, not kelly. Backgrounds should feel lit from within, not from harsh contrast. High key overall.

- **Waxing gibbous (76% illuminated)** → Visual contrast can be present but not extreme. Not a full-moon dramatic moment. A gentle asymmetry in contrast—some areas brighter, some softer, but nothing harsh.

- **My Morning Jacket + Wet Leg** → Personality texture only. MMJ suggests warmth and organic looseness; Wet Leg suggests wit and precision. Fold both in subtly through generous but precise spacing; let the type breathe but don't be sloppy.

- **Awwwards Design Trend Observation** → The three sites show strong use of immersive storytelling (TBU's 3D map), clear philosophical through-lines (peace put's brand coherence), and accessible complexity (NRG turning energy topics engaging). Borrow the *clarity* and *journey* sensibility, not the visual flash.

- **Hacker News cultural moment** → ("The West Forgot How to Make Things") + GitHub trending (Claude/AI tooling) suggests a subtle undercurrent of *craft and agency* in the air. This can inform project presentation—show not just what was made, but *how* and *why it matters*. But don't be on-the-nose about it. The brief is moderate influence (5/10), so this is texture, not dominance.

---

## Palette Direction

Bright, desaturated, and cool-warm balanced. Think spring morning light—high luminosity, low saturation. Lead with soft neutrals (near-whites, warm grays, possibly a pale sage or celadon as an accent). The palette should feel like it's been gently bleached by 13+ hours of daylight. Contrast should exist but never harsh; this is a high-key day. If an accent color is needed, it should be muted and purposeful—not decorative. The waxing gibbous moon is still building toward fullness, so allow subtle asymmetry in brightness across the page, but keep everything luminous. No darks. No saturation for saturation's sake.
