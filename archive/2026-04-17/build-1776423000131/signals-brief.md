# Signals Brief — 2026-04-17

# Creative Brief: doug-march.com
**Date:** 2026-04-17 | **Signal Weight:** 5/10 (Moderate Influence)

---

## Mood

Friday afternoon energy meets the pull of something quieter—a new moon whispers restraint while spring insists on renewal. There's a productive tension here: the day wants momentum (it's Friday, the Tigers won, tech culture is buzzing with Claude Opus), but the lunar phase and that Deng quote about limits suggest a moment to pause before the weekend rush. Hold both. The design should feel like forward motion *paused at a threshold*—not celebrating yet, not resting yet. Alert but not frantic.

---

## Composition Direction

**Scroll with Specimen tendencies.** Spring and moderate daylight hours (13.1 hours of usable daylight) call for breathing room and vertical unfurling rather than dense compression. The Scroll archetype lets information reveal itself gradually as the user moves down the page, which matches the Friday-afternoon state of mind. However, Specimen's commitment to singular focal points should govern the type scale and visual hierarchy—let one element dominate each viewport section before releasing to the next, rather than competing for attention. This is a day for *sequential discovery*, not simultaneous overwhelm.

---

## Typography Direction

Generous vertical rhythm with controlled tension. Headings should sit quietly at first—not whispers, but not shouts either—giving the reader permission to scroll into them rather than demanding attention from the viewport edge. Letter-spacing should be neutral to slightly open (influenced by Radiohead's angular precision in the music signal, but tempered by the new moon's minimalism). Line height should be tall enough to feel spacious but not luxurious—this is Friday afternoon, not a weekend escape. Let type weight carry hierarchy rather than size alone; a heavier regular-weight line can anchor without screaming.

---

## Signal Integration

- **Detroit Tigers 10-9 win** → Inline scoreline callout near the top of the page, styled as a casual editorial aside rather than a card or badge. Tone should be matter-of-fact and slightly wry—a win worth noting, not worth fanfare. Keep it compact; it's Friday, not championship Sunday.

- **Claude Opus 4.7 + AI repo surge (HN signal)** → Subtle texture layer: allow code-like visual elements (monospace fragments, line-break rhythms) to appear as micro-accents in whitespace or margins. Don't make it theme the whole page—this is texture you *feel*, not something that announces itself. The tech conversation is happening; reflect its existence without platforming it.

- **New moon (0.011 illumination)** → Reduce overall contrast slightly. Avoid pure black text on pure white. Favor near-blacks on off-whites or slightly desaturated color pairs. The page should feel lit by ambient light, not harsh noon. This is the most important atmospheric signal after season.

- **Spring + 13.1 daylight hours** → Palette should lean toward fresh, unsaturated greens and warm off-whites. High key overall—the page should feel like mid-afternoon light, not dramatic or moody. Saturation should be moderate (not pale pastels, not vivid jewel tones). Think fresh linen, new growth, diffuse sunlight through leaves.

- **Deng quote ("Things cannot forever go downward...")** → Position this as a quiet anchor near the end of the scroll, perhaps as an epigraph or standalone line. It deserves to be *found*, not promoted. The sentiment matches the new moon's introspective energy and the "pause at threshold" mood.

- **Radiohead + Tobin Sprout + Wet Leg** → Influence type precision and angular composition choices subtly. Not visually literal (no band imagery), but let the taste in music inform how tightly or loosely the page breathes. Radiohead's precision → tighter letter-spacing; Wet Leg's lo-fi energy → permission for imperfection in alignment or spacing. Let this be felt in the *personality* of spacing and line-breaking decisions.

- **Friday (not weekend)** → Layout should feel structured and navigable, not relaxed. Composition should have clear visual pathways and purposeful white space, not lazy sprawl. This is productive space, not lounge space.

---

## Palette Direction

Fresh spring green (low saturation, high brightness) as primary accent, paired with warm off-whites and near-blacks—think the color of new leaves backlit by afternoon sun, not vivid kelly green. Contrast should be *moderate*: avoid pure black/white pairs; instead, use near-black (#1a1a1a or warmer equivalent) on off-white (#f9f8f6) to honor the new moon's softer light. Saturation across the board should be restrained—this is spring without sweetness, bright without gaiety. The overall feeling should be calm daylight, not golden hour drama.
