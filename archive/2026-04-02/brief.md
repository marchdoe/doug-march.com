## Claude's Rationale

**Creative Direction:** The brief demanded a Broadsheet archetype serving Thursday's midweek structured density—a columnar, information-rich grid that honors the week in motion. The full moon signal (0.994 illumination) drives the entire palette architecture: near-black page background (#09130E) paired with near-white primary text (#EDF5F0) creates the maximum contrast (≈13:1 ratio) the brief calls for. Spring green (hue 145°) emerges as the primary color signal, appearing only in column headers and the masthead date badge—alive, saturated, never muted. The Fraunces/Outfit pairing encodes Wet Leg's wry precision (wonky optical serif) and The War on Drugs' deliberate clarity (geometric sans), with generous horizontal letter-spacing in headlines ("let air move through them") anchoring the type personality. One specimen accent zone—the Colin Powell quote in the secondary column—breaks the broadsheet's density with 48px vertical padding and a partial 2px spring green left border rule, carved into the grid as the one moment of visual breathing room. The Tigers scoreline callout (tertiary column, single line, #C86438 burnt terracotta) serves as editorial dismissal with wry understatement. Accessibility is non-negotiable: secondary text (#92BFAA) maintains 5.5:1 contrast against the page background, and the entire system avoids pure black/white in favor of tinted near-neutrals derived from the spring green hue.
