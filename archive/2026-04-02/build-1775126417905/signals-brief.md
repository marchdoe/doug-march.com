# Signals Brief — 2026-04-02

# Creative Brief: doug-march.com
**April 2, 2026**

---

## Mood

Spring's arrival is pulling everything toward renewal and openness—a full moon amplifies clarity and contrast—yet the afternoon is already three-quarters complete, and Detroit's Tigers stumbled last night. There's a peculiar tension here: **bright, lengthening daylight meets a midweek loss; festive seasonal energy meets the grind of Thursday**. The design should feel awake and forward-moving (spring, full moon illumination, nearly half the daylight year behind us), but with a wry acknowledgment that momentum can stall. This is not a day to resolve the contradiction. Hold both the optimism and the slight deflation simultaneously—like looking at a flower while checking yesterday's score.

---

## Composition Direction

**Broadsheet with Specimen accents.**

Thursday midweek calls for structured density—a broadsheet's columnar, information-rich grid acknowledges that the week is in motion and Doug's work/interests deserve clear, organized presentation. But the full moon's high illumination and spring's arrival warrant occasional moments of visual breathing room (Specimen's dramatic white space). Use the broadsheet as the spine—clear hierarchy, multiple focal points—but carve out one or two quiet, well-lit specimen moments where a single element (a headline, a note, a striking detail) gets breathing room and contrast.

---

## Typography Direction

**Type should be clean and legible with generous internal spacing, but compressed horizontally.** The broadsheet structure demands strict hierarchy through scale differentiation, but spring's freshness should prevent rigidity—use open letter-spacing in headlines (let air move through them) while keeping line-height tight enough to feel purposeful rather than luxurious. Headings should speak clearly without shouting; they're authoritative without aggression. Body text should feel navigable, like moving through a newspaper you actually want to read.

---

## Signal Integration

- **Full moon (0.994 illumination) → High contrast, dramatic lighting.** The page should feel maximally lit and clear. Use sharp blacks and clean whites as anchors; avoid muddy mid-tones. This is the strongest felt signal—someone landing on the site should immediately sense clarity and visibility.

- **Spring season + April daylight (12.5 hrs) → Fresh, saturated greens and nascent yellows in accent palette.** Not pastel; these should feel alive and recently unfurled. Spring is the PRIMARY color signal today; lean into it.

- **Detroit Tigers 0-1 loss → Small, editorial dismissal element.** Not a banner, not a card. A single-line scoreline callout with slightly compressed or italic treatment—acknowledge the loss with wry understatement. Tuck it into a marginal position (sidebar, footer, or inline with other sports notes). Tone: "noted and moving on."

- **Thursday / off-season (other Detroit teams) → Structured, organized layout grid.** The broadsheet archetype handles this; use it to anchor the layout rhythm. No sprawling, no leisure. Tidy columns, clear separations.

- **Upcoming Easter (3 days) & Masters Tournament (7 days) → Subtle thematic texture, not a feature.** Easter is close enough to acknowledge softly—perhaps a single Easter egg shape or bloom detail as a texture element, not a dominating visual. Masters is further out; let it breathe. Neither should interrupt the primary spring/full-moon clarity.

- **Wet Leg + The War on Drugs on rotation → Type personality hint.** Wet Leg's wry, post-punk sensibility + TWOD's deliberate, atmospheric approach = use typography that's both precise and slightly alive. Not mechanical; not precious. Let there be intent and edge in the letterforms.

- **Colin Powell quote (kindness theme) → Optional quiet feature.** If it lands naturally in the layout, pull it as a featured quote or callout—it's thematically coherent with spring's gentle renewal. If it feels forced, leave it subtle. Don't force the quote into prominence.

- **Hacker News: Artemis II launch (923 points, dominant story) → Tech-forward atmospheric texture, not a news feature.** This is a cultural signal of Doug's environment, not a headline on his personal site. Let it inform the personality (perhaps code-aesthetic details, orbital geometry in background texture, upward motion) but don't build a news section around it.

---

## Palette Direction

**High contrast, spring-forward, with cool-bright dominance.** The full moon demands sharp blacks and bright whites as structural anchors. Saturate spring greens and fresh yellows for accents—these should feel verdant and alive, not muted. Avoid warm browns or earth tones; spring hasn't settled into that yet. The overall temperature is **cool-bright** (full moon clarity + early spring light), with warm accents reserved for small moments of editorial flourish (the Tigers callout could have a touch of wry warmth, for instance). Contrast should be high—this is a day of visibility and clear sight lines. Restraint in hue count (perhaps 3-4 colors total: black, white, green, one accent) keeps the broadsheet readable and the full moon's clarity undimmed.
