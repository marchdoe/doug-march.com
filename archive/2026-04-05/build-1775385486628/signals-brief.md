# Signals Brief — 2026-04-05

# Creative Brief: doug-march.com
## 2026-04-05

---

## Mood

Easter Sunday arrives with the site in a contemplative, weekend-spacious mood—spring-fresh and unhurried. But there's a productive undercurrent: the Hacker News feed is alive with genuine novelty (a GPU-building game at 756 points; language compilers; open-source tools), while a multi-language GitHub presence suggests active, curious work happening quietly in the background. Hold this tension: the page should feel restful and expansive (it's Sunday, the moon is high and bright at 85% illumination), yet textured with intellectual energy and forward momentum. The quote—"Each day your life grows a day shorter. Make every move count"—lands hard on a weekend. Let it anchor the day's design not as a motivational banner, but as a quiet requirement: nothing here should feel wasted or decorative.

---

## Composition Direction

**Specimen with Gallery Wall energy.** 

A Specimen archetype gives us permission to showcase work (the GitHub repos, the Hacker News discovery) with generous breathing room and clear hierarchy—each item gets its own contained moment. The Gallery Wall energy comes from Sunday's spaciousness and the weekend's natural desire to browse rather than scan; the layout should invite lingering, not rushing. This avoids the density of a Broadsheet (which would feel too Monday-morning) and the relentless scroll of a Scroll (which would undercut the contemplative mood).

---

## Typography Direction

Generous and precise. Leading should be open—avoid crowding on a day that asks for reflection. Headings should whisper rather than shout (the Radiohead influence here: angular letterspacing, tight but not aggressive, suggesting clarity without aggression). Body text should breathe. The quote deserves its own typographic moment—not a callout box, but a considered pause in the layout, possibly in a different scale or weight than the primary voice. Let Ming-Dao Deng's words feel earned, not inserted.

---

## Signal Integration

- **Hacker News: GPU game at 756 points** → Feature as a quiet editorial highlight (not a banner). Treat as a "worth your time" discovery callout, positioned as a specimen-level item that breaks the standard grid slightly. Tone: genuine recommendation, not hype. This is the day's cultural temperature made visible.

- **Easter (holiday)** → Light thematic accent only. Not Easter-egg hunts or pastel explosions. Instead: acknowledge the day's ceremonial weight through subtle framing (perhaps a "spring renewal" conceptual throughline in how projects or work are presented). Keep it grown-up and reflective.

- **Detroit Pistons win (116-93)** → Inline micro-celebration only. A small, confident scoreline callout near whatever sports/personal summary exists. No card, no banner. The win happened yesterday; it's a fact, not today's headline.

- **Detroit Red Wings loss (1-4)** → Spare it editorial dismissal. Don't feature. If included at all, tuck it as a matter-of-fact data point—the kind of score you acknowledge and move past.

- **Radiohead + My Morning Jacket on rotation** → Manifest as typographic precision and warmth simultaneously. Radiohead → tight letter-spacing, angular precision in how information is laid out. MMJ → warmth in color choices (spring greens, golden hour undertones). These are not in conflict; they blend as personality, not as design problem.

- **Masters Tournament in 4 days** → Mention as an upcoming cultural marker, not a feature. A small "watch for" note, perhaps in a secondary hierarchy. Golf context matters here; it's noted but not dominant.

- **Waning gibbous moon (85% illumination, day 18 of cycle)** → High contrast is earned. The moon is still bright; the page can afford strong light/dark relationships. Use this to create visual drama in the specimen layout—let some items cast subtle shadows, let negative space feel intentional rather than empty.

---

## Palette Direction

Spring saturation with restraint: fresh greens and warm golds (influenced by April and the 12.6 hours of daylight), but not candy-bright. The waning moon and the contemplative mood ask for sophisticated restraint—think spring foliage after rain, not Easter pastel. Contrast should be moderate-to-high (the moon permits it), but achieved through value difference more than saturation difference. Avoid muddy tones; spring demands clarity. One accent color maximum, used sparingly for editorial callouts (the HN discovery, perhaps a single project highlight). The overall feel: warm but measured, fresh but composed.
