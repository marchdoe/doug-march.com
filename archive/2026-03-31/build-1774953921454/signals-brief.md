# Signals Brief — 2026-03-31

# Creative Brief: doug-march.com
## March 31, 2026

---

## Mood

Today is a threshold moment—literally the last breath of March before spring fully arrives, and figuratively a day caught between two states: full moon brilliance overhead, but early evening already setting in. There's an underlying unease in the air (two sports losses, security breaches dominating tech discourse), yet the quote about planting seeds in souls suggests quiet, deliberate growth. Design this as **hopeful tension held within restraint**—the site should feel poised and intentional, not panicked. Avoid resolving the conflict between the full moon's dramatic visibility and the day's underlying anxiety into either brightness or darkness; instead, let the design hold both: high contrast where it matters, but compressed and deliberate in its execution.

---

## Composition Direction

**Specimen with Scroll cadence.** 

Today calls for a highly curated, deliberately-paced presentation—the Specimen archetype's obsessive focus on discrete elements speaks to both the full moon's isolation in the sky and the careful documentation impulse evident in today's design references (*The Lookback* as a capsule; *The Extraordinary Lab* as precision engineering). But Specimen alone risks feeling static on a day of unresolved tensions. Layer in Scroll's rhythm to move viewers through these isolated moments with intention, allowing gravity and pacing to do the emotional work rather than density. This hybrid lets you showcase Doug's work as careful, considered pieces rather than a comprehensive dump—appropriate for a Tuesday when energy is moderate and audiences are mid-week attentive.

---

## Typography Direction

**Restrained hierarchy with dramatic scale contrast; precision over generosity.**

Headings should operate at extremes—either quite large and confident (to match the full moon's 98% illumination and the Rumi quote's certainty) or quite small and whispered (for editorial asides and loss-related callouts). Mid-range type should be tight: letter-spacing crisp and intentional, line-height efficient but not cramped. This mirrors the Radiohead/My Morning Jacket pairing in the music signal—angular precision meeting emotional depth. Avoid warm, generous spacing; today demands clarity and deliberation over comfort.

---

## Signal Integration

- **Full moon (98% illumination) → High contrast, dramatic shadows.** The page should feel lit by something bright and slightly eerie. Use stark value separation between foreground and background; avoid mid-tone transitions. This is the dominant visual signal and should be felt immediately upon landing.

- **Spring season + 12.4 daylight hours → Fresh but not saturated palette; generous light but not harsh.** Greens and warm neutrals are appropriate, but keep saturation moderate. The lengthening day should make the layout feel more open than yesterday, but don't overdo it—we're only 6 days from Easter, and anticipation should feel restrained.

- **Tuesday (weekday, mid-cycle energy) → Structured, rhythmic layout with moderate density.** Not sparse, not overwhelming. Let the Specimen + Scroll approach create a steady beat rather than scattered exploration.

- **Detroit Pistons & Tigers losses (110-114, 6-9) → Editorial callouts with dismissive, understated tone.** Don't bury these, but don't celebrate them either. Small inline annotations or footnote-style treatments that acknowledge the losses with a shrug—wry, brief, moving on. Treat them as texture, not story.

- **Rumi quote ("What is planted in each person's soul will sprout") → Feature as a quiet anchor, likely in footer or as a chapter transition.** This resonates deeply with March-to-April threshold energy and the "planting" metaphor. Position it where it lands like a seed—understated, but impossible to miss on close reading. Don't overdesign it.

- **Awwwards references (Lookback, Lab, Overlake) → Inform composition density and documentation approach, not color or type.** These three sites share a documentation-and-storytelling impulse. Borrow their curatorial restraint: let white space work harder than illustration. Favor clear image presentation over decorative overlays.

- **Hacker News security focus (Axios/Claude leaks dominating discourse) → Subtle texture of caution, not alarmism.** If implemented, use this as a very light undercurrent—perhaps a slight visual "tension" in how certain UI elements interact, or a wry aside in copy, but never as a dark warning banner. Acknowledge the moment's unease without amplifying it.

- **GitHub activity (Python, TypeScript, HTML repos—technical, pragmatic) → Type treatment hint toward precision and code-aesthetic.** No actual code blocks needed, but the signal suggests a user who values practical clarity over flourish. Keep type families to 2 maximum; favor monospace for any functional text; no decorative type.

## Palette Direction

**High contrast cool-neutrals anchored by spring greens; restrained saturation; dramatic value separation.**

The full moon dominates the color temperature here—expect strong blacks/near-blacks and very bright neutrals/whites as a foundation, with spring greens introduced as an accent (not the primary) to honor the season. Avoid warm ochres or golden tones; instead, let any warmth come from off-whites and light grays. Saturation should remain controlled across the board—this is not a vibrant spring design, it's a *precise* spring design. The high contrast requirement (driven by the moon and the need to hold visual tension) means shadows should be deep and light areas should be crisp, with minimal mid-tones. Think archival photograph + spring garden, not floral abundance.
